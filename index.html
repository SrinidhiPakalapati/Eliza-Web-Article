Introduction:

Long befor modern AI assistents existed,there was a simple computer program called ELIZA. Created in 1966 ELIZA became one of the earlist chatbots ever developed and showed that computers could imitate human conversations. Even through it was very basic compared to today's AI systems, ELIZA played an improtant role in the history of Artificial Intelligence.



Who created ELIZA?

ELIZA was created by Joseph Weizenbaum at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology(MIT). He designed the program as an experiment to study communication between humans and machines.

The chatbot was named after "Eliza Doolittle", a character from the famous play Pygmalion, because the program could 'learn' to speak different style.



How did ELIZA work?

ELIZA did not actually understand language like modern AI. Instead, it used pattern matching and simple rules to respond to user.

For example:

User: "I am feeling sad."

Eliza: "Why are you feeling sad?"

The program searched for keywords in the sentenc and genereated a response based on pre-written templates. One of the famous versions acted like therapist,encoraging users to continue talking.



Why was ELIZA important?

ELIZA was improtant because it proved that humans could feel emotionally to the machine, even when the machine did not truly understand them.

Many people who have talked to ELIZA believed that it was intelligent.This suprised even Joseph Weizenbaum himeself. He later warned people not to trust computers with human emotions and decisions.

ELIZA also insprired future development in:

  • Chatbots
  • Natural Language Processing (NLP)
  • Conversational AI
  • Virtual assistents
